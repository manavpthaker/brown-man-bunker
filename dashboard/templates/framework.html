Risk Analysis Framework Overview

The Brown Man Bunker monitoring system tracks indicators across six hypothesis categories (H1-H6) that represent potential systemic risks to household stability. Each hypothesis encompasses specific threat vectors that could disrupt normal life patterns.

The Six Risk Hypotheses

H1: Financial System Instability

Monitors: Treasury market volatility, mBridge currency adoption

Risk: Banking crisis, currency devaluation, loss of savings

Impact: Your money becomes inaccessible or worthless, credit freezes, economic collapse

H2: Geopolitical Conflicts

Monitors: Taiwan tensions, Strait of Hormuz shipping risk, military AI deployment

Risk: Supply chain disruption, energy crisis, potential for wider conflict

Impact: Critical goods unavailable, fuel shortages, economic disruption

H3: AI/Technology Disruption

Monitors: AGI development milestones, labor displacement rates, cyber vulnerabilities

Risk: Mass unemployment, critical infrastructure attacks, social upheaval

Impact: Job obsolescence, system failures, need for new economic models

H4: Social Cohesion Breakdown

Monitors: Immigration detention rates, school closures, luxury market collapse

Risk: Civil unrest, community breakdown, increased crime

Impact: Unsafe neighborhoods, disrupted services, need for self-reliance

H5: Infrastructure Failure

Monitors: Power grid outages, pharmacy shortages, critical system vulnerabilities

Risk: Essential services offline, medication unavailable, cascading failures

Impact: No power/water/heat, medical emergencies, inability to work

H6: Economic Collapse

Monitors: Unemployment claims, GDP growth, food inflation

Risk: Job loss, inability to afford basics, recession/depression

Impact: Can't pay bills, food insecurity, housing crisis

Response Protocol: TIGHTEN-UP

When 3 or more indicators reach RED status simultaneously, the system triggers a TIGHTEN-UP alert. This is your signal to implement basic preparedness measures:

  1. Top off all vehicle fuel tanks
  2. Withdraw cash in small bills ($300-500)
  3. Charge all devices and backup power banks
  4. Check food, water, and medication supplies
  5. Review emergency contact information
  6. Test backup communication methods
  7. Ensure important documents are accessible

Remember: This is not about panic, but prudent preparation. Most alerts will resolve without incident, but being prepared ensures your family's comfort during disruptions.

Resilience Phases

Based on your preparedness level, you can progress through phases 0-9:

  • Phase 0: Foundation - Skills and mindset
  • Phase 1: 72-hour emergency supplies
  • Phase 2: Communications and digital resilience
  • Phase 3: Air quality and mobile communications
  • Phase 4: Water security and storage
  • Phase 5: Backup power preparation
  • Phase 6: Secure shelter improvements
  • Phase 7: Standby generator installation
  • Phase 8: Rainwater collection and electrical circuits
  • Phase 9: Optional safe room construction
